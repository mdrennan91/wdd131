Washington State, US

Washington state, located in the Pacific Northwest of the United States, is distinguished by its diverse landscapes, ranging from the Pacific coastline to mountain ranges and fertile farmlands. Approximately 60% of Washington's residents live in the Seattle metropolitan area, the center of transportation, business, and industry, and home to an internationally renowned arts scene. The state is a leading lumber producer and is famous for its apples, cherries, and the production of wine. Washington's significant contributions to technology, aerospace, and a booming economy are juxtaposed with its vast areas of untouched wilderness, including national parks like Olympic and Mount Rainier.
